Checkout Experience at Juvo

The content on this page is my own and does not necessarily reflect the views of Juvo.

While I cannot fully showcase my design work and process here to remain in compliance with my NDA, you can still read an overview of my time at Juvo below. If you’re interested in learning more, please send me an email.

Overview

Role: Design Technologist Intern
Timeframe: May–August 2019 (10 weeks)
Tools: Figma, XCode, InVision Studio, After Effects

Key contributions:

1. Proposed new user checkout interactions for the Juvo mobile app (JuvoUP)

2. Reimagined the Juvo Design System

3. Generated conversation surrounding interaction design (ID) and design in general internally at Juvo

Process

My work at Juvo followed an iterative timeline that began with a careful user research investigation of the product deployments, followed by a series of three research, design and engineering (RDE) two-week sprints, included multiple Product and Design System focus group sessions, and concluded with a final presentation with prototype deliverables to the entire team of Juvo HQ in SF.

Impact

1. New user checkout interactions

I explored interaction design principles and best practices to improve the user experience of making a purchase and seeing the purchase confirmation message in the JuvoUP mobile app—critical points in the user journey of Juvo’s primary mobile app offering.

2. Reimagined the Juvo Design System

I joined Juvo when the Product and Design teams were convinced of the merits of a design system, but the existing design system was not functioning seamlessly cross-functionally. With members of Juvo Design in focus group sessions, I dissected the communication & technical barriers hindering uptake within Juvo before offering suggestions based on my research of other well-known design systems.

Moreover, I authored documentation of animation, transitions, and micro-animations based on my own work in the existing Juvo Design System.

3. Sparked design conversation at Juvo

Crafting a beautiful product and user experience is impossible unless everyone who works on the product believes in the design process. It was a welcome surprise that my project and time at Juvo would spark so much interest and conversation among all teams at Juvo HQ, including Engineering, about interaction design and the general “feel” of the app.
