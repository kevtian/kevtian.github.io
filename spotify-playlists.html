Playlist Experience Redesign - Spotify

Motive

I absolutely love listening to music on Spotify. The predictions are great, the quantity of content is beyond adequate, and most of the user experience is streamlined, pleasant, and intuitive. This was a personal project to familiarize myself with InVision and to practice picking apart basic experience flaws in a popular product.

How

To produce the sketches, I used InVision Freehand to wireframe the current Spotify design and brainstorm the new design for a better playlist experience.

Getting Started

I began this project by wireframing the current Spotify design and breaking down the current "add-to playlist" experience in a low-fidelity view on Freehand by InVision. I considered my updated user experience target audience as the listeners who download songs in batches, but likely do not add the songs to playlists immediately after.

Overcoming Problems

Deciding how to best implement the select-multiple feature of the interface was a challenging decision.

Another interesting challenge was considering why I am designing the updated experience the way I am. In order to ensure a more streamlined experience, saving the user many button clicks, I wanted to generate a solution to add multiple songs to a playlist easily. However, I realized that this feature could also be easily implemented for "add-to queue" as well. Both of these.

Final Product

The final design of the low-fidelity Spotify add-to playlist user experience is reasonable for everyday users. The click-and-hold on the three-dot menu for each specific song is an approachable, understandable interface which can be implemented with Apple Force Touch.

Takeaways

  • Redesigning an already great user experience seemed very challenging at first. However, after identifying a simple feature still unavailable on the live version of Spotify mobile, it became much easier to imagine how the user experience of Spotify could be improved.
